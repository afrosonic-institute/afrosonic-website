The Archive

This is where the work lives. The interviews with musicians who shaped Nigerian popular culture. The growing collection of vinyl, cassettes, and recordings that might not exist in another decade. Afrosonic's events exist to keep this music in the air. The archive exists to keep it in the record.

PHYSICAL COLLECTION

Records at risk.

Nigerian popular music from the 1950s to the early 2000s was pressed on vinyl, recorded on cassette, and broadcast on reel-to-reel. Much of it was never digitised. Afrosonic is building a physical archive — every donated item catalogued, stored, and where possible digitised.

If you have a collection, get in touch before sending anything.
